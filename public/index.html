Calm the Calamities

Game Design Document

by David Silverman, Wei Hang Hong, and Jiwon Jang

Introduction

This document describes a game called “Calm The Calamities”, which is a top-down, RPG/puzzle game in which you use different elemental abilities to save a world that you have created. This game employs many basic 2D game techniques such as tiled backgrounds, collision detection, and efficient memory management.

Technology

Calm The Calamities will be developed as a web application using the Wolfie2D game engine, created by Richard McKenna. Original music and sounds will be created using Petaporon, and original artwork will be created using Piskel and Tiled.

Backstory

You are a young god who just finished creating your first world. You feel tired after making the world so you decided to take a nap. The nap turns into a millennium long slumber. While you were asleep, your powers leaked out from your body into your world. They took the shape of violent spirits, creating all kinds of natural disasters throughout your world.

Objective

Your job is to navigate through a series of puzzles, navigating around various elements (“calamities”) that serve as obstacles, and defeating the boss spirits to gain your elemental powers back.

Each level consists of two rooms, a puzzle room and a boss room. The level is focused around a certain calamity (e.g. earth, wind, etc), although multiple calamities can be present in a level. You can use your elemental powers to assist you as well in this area. Once you clear the puzzle room, you will fight a boss spirit that can damage you, and you must defeat it to gain its power and move on to the next level.

Gameplay

This game will play as a top-down puzzle RPG. In each level, you will use any elemental powers you have gained (you will only have your fists at the start) to solve the puzzle and defeat the boss. For each element/calamity, they can serve as an obstacle which you can interact with if it is in front of you, and which you can place yourself if you have defeated the boss spirit of that element.

Calamity Obstacle Description Interaction (J Key) Placing (K Key)
Earth Comes in the form of small, medium, or large rocks that can be pushed by the player Push a rock Place the rock in the tile in front of you
Wind Travels in one of four directions, pushing the player in said direction Turn the wind tile into an airstream that extends multiple tiles Place wind in the tile in front of you
Water Comes in the form of shallow or deep waters that prevent player movement, or water bubbles that can act as a player shield Turn a water bubble into a wave that extends multiple tiles Place a water bubble in the tile in front of you
Fire Spreads across tiles to light torches or kill the player Ignite a fire spark to burn the tile in front of it Place a fire spark in the tile in front of you
Ice Slippery tiles that slide the player. Being near ice for too long can also kill the player N/A N/A

Controls

This game will be played using a keyboard, with a mouse necessary to click on various menus. Once you have started the game, use the following:

  • W - Move Up
  • A - Move Left
  • S - Move Down
  • D - Move Right
  • 1,2,3,4,5,6 - Select Elements
  • J - Interact with Element
  • K - Place Selected Element
  • ESCAPE - Pause Game

Artwork

Original artwork will be created for this game in the forms of sprites and tiles using Piskel and Tiled. The following needs to be created:

  • The God - Animated sprite, with animations for:
    • Moving
    • Interacting with Element
    • Placing an Element
    • Taking Damage
    • Dying
  • Boss Spirits - Animated sprite, with animations for:
    • Moving
    • Attacking
    • Taking Damage
    • Dying
  • Tiles - For use in 5 puzzle rooms and 5 boss rooms. Tiles include:
    • Floor
    • Ground
    • Walls
    • Elements/Obstacles

Sound Effects

Original sound effects will be created for this game using Petaporon. They will correspond to the following events:

  • Interacting with Element
  • Placing an Element
  • Taking Damage (God/Boss)
  • Dying (God/Boss)
  • Gaining a Power (End of Level)

Music

Original music will be created for this game using Petaporon.
